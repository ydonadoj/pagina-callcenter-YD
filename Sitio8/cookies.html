01 What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites install in the user's browser when they visit. These files allow the site to remember information about the visit, such as preferred language and other settings, in order to facilitate navigation and improve the user experience.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies: Automatically deleted when the user closes the browser.
  • Persistent cookies: Remain on the device for a set period or until the user manually deletes them.
  • First-party cookies: Installed directly by the website being visited.
  • Third-party cookies: Installed by external services integrated into the site, such as analytics tools.

The website uses Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google LLC, to understand how users interact with the site and continuously improve its content and usability. Google Analytics installs the following cookies:

Cookie Type Data collected Purpose Expiration
_ga Persistent / Third-party Anonymous unique user identifier; pages visited; time on site; approximate location (country/city); device type and browser. Distinguish unique users and generate statistical usage reports for the site. 2 years

04 Functional cookies — Contact form

The contact form available on our website requires data voluntarily entered by the user: name, email address, and message. To ensure the correct functioning of the form, the site may temporarily store this information in the browser's session storage.

  • This data is used exclusively to process and send the user's contact request to Alliance Contact Solutions.
  • It is not shared with third parties, is not used for advertising purposes, and is not permanently stored on the user's device.
  • Information entered in the form is automatically deleted when the browser session is closed.

Legal basis: The processing of contact form data is based on the technical necessity of fulfilling the user's request (contact or quote) and on the consent given when completing and submitting the form, in accordance with Colombian Law 1581 of 2012.

05 Managing cookies by browser

Users can configure their browser to accept, reject, or delete cookies at any time. Below are the steps for managing cookies in the most common browsers:

🌎 Google Chrome

  1. Click the ⋮ menu (three dots) in the upper right corner.
  2. Select Settings.
  3. Go to Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data.
  4. Choose your preferred management option or delete specific cookies.

🦊 Mozilla Firefox

  1. Click the ☰ menu (three lines) in the upper right corner.
  2. Select Settings.
  3. Go to Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data.
  4. Select your protection level or manually delete cookies.

🍎 Apple Safari

  1. Go to Safari → Preferences (on Mac) or Settings (on iOS).
  2. Select the Privacy tab.
  3. Enable Block all cookies or manage website data.
  4. Click Manage Website Data to delete specific cookies.

🎉 Microsoft Edge

  1. Click the ⋯ menu (three dots) in the upper right corner.
  2. Select Settings.
  3. Go to Privacy, search, and services → Cookies and site permissions.
  4. Choose your preferences or delete existing cookies.
Note: Disabling analytical cookies does not affect website functionality. However, disabling contact form cookies may interrupt the submission of requests.
